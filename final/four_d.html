The Four D's

What are the Four D's?

The four D's are Dyslexia, Dysgraphia, Dyscalculia, and Dyspraxia

The four D's are often just described as Dyslexia. But there is more then JUST Dyslexia. Dyslexia is just the one that people most often refere to because they dont know all of the different types event though Dyslexia is its own. There are four different types called The four D's.

Dyslexia

People with Dyslexia have a harder time learning to read and spell. Even after learning to read and spell they still had a harder time reading and spelling then the average person.

Analogy

(think of these learning differences as a busy city)

Dyslexia is like road signs being written in a confusing font, and reading them takes extra effort.

Dysgraphia

Affects the learning of writing processes, spelling, legibility, word spacing and sizing. It impairs writing ability and fine motor skills

Analogy

(think of these learning differences as a busy city)

Dysgraphia is like trying to write directions with a broken pen; its hard to get words onto paper smoothly.

Dyscalculia

Affects the ability to acquire arithmetical skills, but also the understanding of number concepts, facts and procedures.

Analogy

(think of these learning differences as a busy city)

Dyscalculia is like maps with roads that dont quite connect; numbers and calculations feel disorganized.

Dyspraxia

(also known as DCD) Developmental Coordination Disorder. It affects both fine and gross motor skills, such as writing or sewing, and balance and coordination.

Analogy

(think of these learning differences as a busy city)

Dyspraxia is like sidewalks being uneven; physical movements and coordination take extra conscious effort.
